Entries for Statues

View the statues of Jose Rizal erected worldwide as lasting symbols of his heroism and ideals.

UNITED STATES


Located at Lincoln Park, Chicago it was unveiled on June 19,1999 through the initiative of the Order of the Knights of Rizal and various Filipino-American community organizations. Dr. Rizal shortly visited Chicago on May 11, 1888, arriving at 8:14 a.m. and departing for New York City at 8:25 p.m. that same day. This monument is one of the two life size monuments of Dr. Jose Rizal in the United States. Antonio “Lito” Mondejar created the monument, it features a bronze sculpture with a black granite base, a replica of the one found in Dapitan. A parade was held at its unveiling on 1999.








MADRID


The José Rizal statue in Madrid, Spain, is located along Avenida de Filipinas near the Philippine Embassy. It was unveiled in 1996 to honor Rizal’s legacy and his time in Madrid, where he studied and joined the Propaganda Movement. oo The bronze statue shows Rizal holding a book, symbolizing his intellect and fight for peaceful reforms. It stands as a tribute to his contributions and a reminder of the strong historical ties between the Philippines and Spain. (Jose Rizal’s Walking Tour Madrid | Philippine Embassy Madrid, n.d.)







PARIS


The monument of Rizal in France is located in Jose Rizal’s Place. This bust was created on June 23, 2022, as a tribute to his courage to fight for Philippine independence and human rights, and was supported by government officials and the Filipino community (Department of Foreign Affairs, 2022; Balikbayan Magazine, 2022).






NETHERLANDS


The bust monument of Jose Rizal in the Netherlands is located at the Philippine Embassy grounds in The Hague, Netherlands. It was created in Pampanga and donated by the Knights of Rizal – The Hague Chapter to acknowledge Rizal’s legacy and teachings (BusinessMirror, 2023; Department of Foreign Affairs, 2023b).






JAPAN


The monument of Jose Rizal in Tokyo, Japan is located along the perimeter trail of Hibiya Park near the Imperial Palace in Tokyo. This bust was installed during the Centennial Anniversary of Philippine Independence in 1998, and can be found beside Rizal’s marker (Young, 2019; Department of Foreign Affairs, 2023a).






CANADA


The bust monument of Rizal in Canada was presented to the Ville de Montréal in 1998 as a celebration of the proclamation of Philippine independence. It is located at Parc Mackenzie - King in Montreal, placed on a granite pedestal and features French, English, and Tagalog inscriptions as an acknowledgment of Rizal’s role in the country’s independence (De Leon Imao, 1999; Department of Foreign Affairs, 2019).






MARKHAM, ONTARIO, CANADA


This monument is the latest addition to Markham’s public art collection created by a partnership between the Filipino Canadian community of Markham and the city as part of the celebration of 121 years of Philippine Independence. The monument was unveiled on June 10, 2019 which Mogado notes that the sculpture “will honour a man who spent his lifetime advocating for ideas and principles that inspire generations of freedom fighters around the world”. During the unveiling, it was attended by Markham Mayor Frank Scarpitto, former councilor Alex Chiu and Philippine Deputy Consul General Bernadette Therese C. Fernandez. The sculptor, Ignacio “Mogi” Mogado Jr., made of half-inch thick bronze cast over a base of molded plaster of paris, the statue rests on a plinth of top grade granite. The monument, located in Boxgrove Community Park, has Rizal positioned just across the street where Rizal Avenue begins.






HONOLULU, HAWAII


Sculptor Carl Ruiz dedicated a life sized statue of Dr. Jose P. Rizal on the corner of North Beretania Street and College Walk, on December 30, 1983. The 5-foot, 6-inch cement, iron and bronze state commemorates Rizal’s contribution to the revolution. The Filipina Society of Hawaii donated the statue in cooperation with the Oahu Filipino Community Council and Laoag City Lions Club (Gobetz, 2010).






CARSON, CALIFORNIA


In December 2004, the community leaders of the Filipino-American community decided to form the Jose P. Rizal Monument Movement which they voted to erect a Rizal statue in California. Eight years later, the 15-ft tall was unveiled at the International Sculpture Garden in Carson. The monument was also created by Filipino artist Toym Imao.






MEXICO CITY


To celebrate the historic ties between the Philippines and Mexico, a striking statue of Dr. Jose Rizal is prominently displayed in a serene area of Lomas de Chapultepec, positioned at a key intersection of Paseo de la Reforma and Montañas Rocallosas Ote. Crafted by Costa Rican-Mexican artist Francisco Zuñiga, this tribute to the Filipino national hero was unveiled on 12 June 1967. Dr. Rizal, who traveled to Mexico in 1890, found deep inspiration in the country's struggle for independence from Spanish rule and held great admiration for the bravery of Mexican revolutionaries such as Miguel Hidalgo and Jose Maria Morelos. His statue serves as a lasting reminder of his role in the push for Filipino freedom from colonial oppression in the late 19th century, perpetually honoring the connection between the two countries (Villareal,2022).
